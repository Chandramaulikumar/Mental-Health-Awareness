Covid-19 Effects on Mental Health

What is Mental Health?

Mental Health is every human's psychological, emotional well-being. It affects how we think, the actions we do based on behaviour, and how we feel emotions.

Our Mental Health affects different aspects of our lives every day, for instance, the way we communicate with others or how we react to certain situations that may arise. How do you know you have a healthy mind? Being mentally stable and able to manage your emotions, having the ability to be optimistic, validating your feelings and allowing yourself to feel negative and positive emotions, and maintaining your social relationships with others and yourself are some of the various characteristics of a healthy mind. With a healthy mind we can attain the goal of a healthy body that will work together and function to their maximum capacity leaving us feeling renewed.

How has Covid-19 affected Mental Health?

Covid-19 has damaged many aspects of our society and in relation to Mental Health, it is no different. Since the Covid-19 Pandemic started people have been filled with anxiety, stress, and sadness; these emotions are let out by everyone in multiple ways unique to a person. In some instances, these negative emotions are just too overwhelming for us to handle and our mind can short circuit and cause our body to react differently than it used to. Research has shown that 4 in 10 adults have reported symptoms of anxiety or depressive disorder. These symptoms can include fatigue, insomnia, loss of appetite, loss of interest in certain activities and more. Studies also show that other than anxiety and depressive disorders, substance use and consumption has increased by 12%, indicating to us that people are turning to alcohol and other drugs to get rid of these overwhelming emotions that are just too much for their mind to handle. Research tells us Covid-19 can also affect the mental health of specific age groups or genders more than others, in this case, it is said that women are having higher rates of anxiety and depression than men.

How has Covid-19 affected Mental Health in Belize?

In the country of Belize, there are Mental Health services that can be provided but only to a certain extent, the topic overall isn’t really talked about one on one in our communities. As a result of little awareness, Mental Health is often discriminated against by most adults and especially by younger age groups. Luckily, UNICEF has touched on some statistics of Mental Health in the Caribbean which counts Belize in, and due to this some people are aware of what it is. Without realizing it, adults in Belize show symptoms of Mental Health issues every day. Many students are now forced to turn to virtual learning which takes a toll on their minds since everything is so new. Students in my High School always share how stressed and anxious they are of what will happen next with the pandemic or if they’ll be able to fully adjust to the workload and rules of online learning. Primary School Students aren’t all equipped with the materials they need for online learning and so they must do the work physically in packages with no guidance from a teacher, leaving parents to teach their kids. Most parents are already stressed and worried how they will pay bills since most prices increased or because they have lost their jobs, and now they must try their best to educate their children in curriculums they know nothing about. All of these situations cause severe symptoms of stress, anxiety, and depression, yet Mental Health is still stigmatized in Belize. The Ministry of Health in Belize had to release an official announcement about how Covid-19 patients should not be discriminated against just because they were tested positive; these patients were unfortunately being harassed with ignorant and degrading comments from others which caused them to feel emotionally trapped and fearful as to why they were receiving such attention.

Ways to Improve/Manage Mental Health

  • Being active by walking or doing other exercises you enjoy
  • Meditating or practicing Yoga to calm yourself
  • Getting adequate amounts of rest and sleep every day
  • Staying connected to those close to you virtually, for example video calling
  • Talk to people you trust about how you're feeling, your feelings matter and are important
  • Do your best to surround yourself with positivity, this includes people or anything else in your life like your favorite objects or quotes
  • Try to pick up a productive hobby you've always wanted to do, for instance, journaling, painting, or learning a new instrument.
  • Set aside a time of the day where you can be calm and just be thankful for the good things in your life.
